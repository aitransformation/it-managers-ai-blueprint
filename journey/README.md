# The 5-Step Journey

This is where most people should start. The journey takes you from wherever you are now to a governed, operational AI setup for your IT function.

Each step is a self-contained phase of work. Do them in order. The foundations matter — skipping ahead creates problems that come back later.

---

## The 5 steps

| Step | File | Focus | When |
|------|------|-------|------|
| 1 | [`01-audit.md`](01-audit.md) | Map the estate, define outcomes, classify data | Before anything else |
| 2 | [`02-foundations.md`](02-foundations.md) | Policy, data hygiene, governance, backup | Before deploying any agent |
| 3 | [`03-skill-up.md`](03-skill-up.md) | AI literacy for you and your team | Before going live |
| 4 | [`04-build.md`](04-build.md) | Deploy agents, OpenBrain, and local infrastructure | Weeks 3–11 |
| 5 | [`05-feedback-loop.md`](05-feedback-loop.md) | Monthly review, research, continuous improvement | Ongoing |

---

## How to use these files

Each step file explains:
- What the step involves
- What you produce
- What you need before starting
- Which blueprint sections to read for depth

The journey files are summaries. The [`blueprint/`](../blueprint/) files are the full standard. When a journey step says "see Section X", that is where you go for the complete guidance.

---

## The founding principle applies throughout

> **AI amplifies people. It does not replace them.**

Every step in this journey is building something that makes the people in your IT function more capable — not something that removes them from the equation. Keep this front and centre, especially when you are explaining the programme to your team.
