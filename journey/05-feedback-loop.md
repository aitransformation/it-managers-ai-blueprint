# Step 5: Feedback Loop

**Monthly review. Research watchlist. Continuous improvement.**

This is not the end of the journey — it is the step that makes the journey continuous. The feedback loop is what prevents the IT programme from calcifying, accumulating technical debt, or drifting from what the business actually needs.

---

## What this step covers

- The monthly review — 90 minutes, structured, documented
- ROI tracking — measuring against baselines from Step 1
- Prompt library and agent performance review
- Research watchlist — staying current without drowning in noise
- Annual blueprint reset

---

## The monthly review

Ninety minutes. Same day each month. It is a protected calendar entry. It does not move without the senior sponsor's explicit agreement.

The review covers eight agenda items (see Section 18 for the full structure):

1. AI Register — changes, new deployments, incidents
2. Data register — classification changes, quality issues
3. ROI tracking — are the numbers moving as expected?
4. Prompt library — prompts due for review, any decay issues
5. Agent performance — failures, unexpected outputs, access issues
6. Security and compliance — CVEs, DPA renewals, compliance items due
7. Team feedback — what is and is not working
8. Next month priorities — the top three

The output is a one-page summary distributed to the senior sponsor within 48 hours.

---

## ROI as a continuous discipline

Baseline measurements were established in Step 1. Now you measure against them.

Every 90 days, calculate ROI for each deployed capability. A positive result confirms value. A negative result triggers a review — either a technical problem to solve, or a prioritisation lesson.

Present ROI to leadership in outcome terms: time recovered, errors reduced, cost avoided. Not agent names. Not model versions.

---

## Research — 30 minutes, three times a week

Structured research is a professional obligation. Not passive reading — active assessment against a consistent benchmark.

Priority areas for March 2026:
- Local model capability — re-evaluate monthly against a benchmark test suite
- MCP adoption — track for all current and prospective tools
- Multi-agent orchestration — develop governance frameworks in advance of deployment readiness
- EU AI Act guidance — reassess AI Register within 30 days of any new publication

Keep a research log in OpenBrain. Tag findings. When something moves from "watch" to "ready to evaluate", run it through the vendor scorecard before acting on it.

---

## Annual blueprint reset

Once per year: re-run outcome mapping against the current business, reassess all deployed systems, review the full AI Register, verify all governance documentation is current.

Schedule this before the end of Q1. It prevents the accumulation of technical debt and ensures the programme continues to serve current business outcomes, not the business outcomes from 18 months ago.

---

## The discipline is the point

The feedback loop works because it is consistent. Monthly reviews that happen reliably accumulate useful data. Research sessions that happen three times a week build genuine expertise. The value is in the repetition, not the individual session.

AI amplifies people. This is the step where that amplification becomes sustainable — because the IT Manager is continuously improving the capability, governing it properly, and staying current in a field that moves fast.
