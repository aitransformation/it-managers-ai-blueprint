# Step 3: Skill Up

**Build AI literacy before you deploy AI.**

Deploying capable AI agents before the IT Manager and team have the skills to verify, govern, and extend them is a risk. The skills come first. The agents follow.

---

## What this step covers

- IT Manager competency development against the five defined standards
- Tier 1 training for all staff
- Tier 2 training for the IT team
- Identifying and briefing internal champions
- Prompt engineering fundamentals
- AI security awareness

---

## The principle

**Skills compound. Tools get replaced.**

The IT Manager who can write a clean, verifiable system prompt and assess an AI output critically is in a far stronger position than the one who can operate a specific tool. Build the skills that will still matter when the tools change.

---

## The five IT Manager competency standards

| Standard | What it looks like in practice |
|----------|-------------------------------|
| **Prompt engineering** | You can write structured prompts with task, context, format, and acceptance criteria. You can diagnose why a prompt is producing weak outputs and fix it. |
| **System architecture** | You can evaluate an agent-built tool's architecture, identify data flow sovereignty issues, and specify integration requirements. |
| **AI output verification** | You treat AI outputs as draft material. You have a verification approach for each output type. You do not act on AI outputs without checking them. |
| **Data literacy** | You have a current, accurate picture of every significant data asset in the organisation — its classification, owner, quality status, and sovereignty requirements. |
| **AI security awareness** | You understand prompt injection, data leakage, and privilege escalation. You assess new capabilities for these risks at design time, not after an incident. |

---

## Training tiers

**Tier 1 — All staff (30 minutes minimum)**
- What AI can and cannot do
- How to recognise AI errors
- What data they can and cannot use with AI tools
- What to do if they are uncertain or something goes wrong

Deliver before any AI-affected tool goes live. Deliver in person. Do not substitute an email or a PDF.

**Tier 2 — IT team (2 hours minimum)**
- Hands-on with deployed tools
- Prompt writing standards
- Output verification requirements
- Incident response when an agent behaves unexpectedly

Tier 2 competency must be demonstrated before unsupervised agent use is permitted. Competency sign-off is required.

**Tier 3 — IT Manager and champions (ongoing)**
- Prompt engineering depth
- Agent operations and governance
- Emerging capabilities and research
- Minimum 2 hours per week, calendar-protected

---

## Internal champions

Identify two people who engage readily with new technology. Brief them before the wider team. Give them test environment access. Formally recognise their role.

Champions are the most effective peer-to-peer support channel. They will also find the practical problems before you go live — which is exactly when you want to find them.

---

## What to focus on first

If your time is limited, prioritise in this order:

1. **AI output verification** — the most immediately useful skill, applicable from day one
2. **Prompt engineering** — needed before you can do anything meaningful with agents
3. **AI security awareness** — needed before any deployment
4. **Data literacy** — ongoing; start with the data assets you are about to connect to AI
5. **System architecture** — deepens as you build; needed for Step 4
