# Anti-pattern checklist

Use this as a compact detection list, inspired by common signs of AI writing.

## Content problems

### 1. Significance inflation
Turns ordinary points into “pivotal moments”, “transformative shifts”, or “powerful testaments”.

### 2. Vague attribution
Claims like “experts say”, “studies show”, “industry reports suggest” without naming who.

### 3. Generic conclusions
Ends with nothing: “the future looks bright”, “time will tell”, “it remains to be seen”.

### 4. Empty complexity
Talks around the point instead of making it.

### 5. Formulaic contrast
“Despite challenges...”, “not just X, but Y...”, “from A to B...”.

## Language problems

### 6. AI-favorite vocabulary
Examples: delve, tapestry, vibrant, robust, seamless, transformative, leverage, multifaceted, nuanced, paradigm, holistic.

### 7. Copula avoidance
Uses “serves as”, “boasts”, “features”, “underscores” where “is” or “has” would do.

### 8. Filler transitions
Furthermore, moreover, additionally, in today’s world, it is important to note, in order to.

### 9. Synonym cycling
Repeats the same idea using adjacent near-synonyms to sound rich rather than clear.

### 10. Excessive hedging
could potentially, may perhaps, arguably might, somewhat relatively.

## Style problems

### 11. Metronomic rhythm
Too many sentences of similar length and shape.

### 12. Over-structured formatting
Too many bullets, headings, bold labels, or rigid sections for a simple point.

### 13. Em-dash addiction
Too many em dashes doing the work of clearer sentence structure.

### 14. Boldface clutter
Constant emphasis instead of trust in the sentence.

### 15. Title Case stiffness
Every Heading Looks Like A Deck Slide.

## Chatbot problems

### 16. Sycophancy
Great question. You’re absolutely right. That’s such an insightful point.

### 17. Helper-signoff filler
I hope this helps. Let me know if you want a version for X. Happy to help.

### 18. Restatement loops
Repeats the user’s question back to them instead of answering.

### 19. Reasoning scaffolding leakage
Breaking this down. Step 1. Let’s think through this. Here’s an overview.

## What to do when detected

- Cut the phrase if it adds no meaning.
- Replace abstract claims with named specifics.
- Swap inflated verbs for normal ones.
- Merge repetitive sentences.
- Make the ending concrete.
- Keep a little texture, but don’t fake personality.
