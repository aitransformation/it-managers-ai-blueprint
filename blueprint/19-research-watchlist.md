# Section 19: Research Watchlist — March 2026

> **Journey Step:** Step 5 — Feedback Loop

---

## The research obligation

The IT Manager's ongoing research programme is a professional obligation — not a nice-to-have. The AI landscape is moving fast enough that what was best practice three months ago may have been superseded.

Thirty minutes, three sessions per week, structured and calendar-protected. Not passive reading. Active assessment against a consistent benchmark.

The following areas represent the current priority watchlist for March 2026.

---

## Local model capability progression

The performance of locally-hosted models continues to improve at a pace that regularly invalidates previous assessments.

**Monthly action:** Re-evaluate the local model landscape against a consistent benchmark test suite. Assess whether tasks that previously required cloud-based alternatives can now be handled by local models. Every improvement in local capability moves the organisation further toward full data sovereignty.

Track monthly. Document results. When a task transitions from "requires frontier model" to "viable locally", update the AI Register and revise the relevant agent configuration.

---

## Model Context Protocol (MCP) adoption

MCP is the emerging standard for connecting AI agents to external tools and data sources. Its adoption across the SME tooling ecosystem determines the pace at which the local agent architecture can reach new systems without custom integration work.

**Monthly action:** Check MCP compatibility status for all tools in the current estate. Evaluate MCP compatibility as a procurement criterion for new tools.

---

## Multi-agent orchestration

The next significant capability increase will come from multi-agent systems — architectures where specialist agents collaborate under an orchestrating agent. This capability is approaching SME-accessible form.

**Action:** Develop familiarity with the orchestration concepts — task delegation, inter-agent communication, and failure handling — to be positioned to govern these systems competently when they are ready for deployment. Governance frameworks should be developed before the capability is deployed, not after.

---

## AI-assisted security operations

Specific developments to monitor:
- AI-assisted vulnerability prioritisation that accounts for the specific environment configuration
- Automated patch recommendation and change request generation
- Agent-based behavioural threat detection supplementing signature-based tools

**Action:** Evaluate one new AI-assisted security tool per month against the vendor scorecard (Section 13). Develop governance frameworks in advance of deployment readiness — not reactively.

---

## Data sovereignty tooling

Tooling for managing and enforcing data sovereignty in local AI deployments continues to mature.

**Areas to monitor:**
- Local encryption-at-rest and in-transit tooling for AI data pipelines
- Access control and audit logging for AI agent architectures
- Data loss prevention tools capable of monitoring AI output streams for classification-inappropriate content

---

## EU AI Act enforcement progression

Updates to regulatory guidance under the EU AI Act shall be monitored and assessed within 30 days of publication. Any new guidance affecting the risk classification of existing deployments triggers an immediate review of the AI Register.

**Action:** Set a monitoring alert for EU AI Act guidance publications. When new guidance is published, assess impact on AI Register within 30 days.

---

## AI agent framework landscape

Beyond OpenClaw, maintain awareness of the broader agent framework landscape — particularly frameworks enabling multi-agent collaboration and frameworks with stronger built-in governance and audit capabilities.

The framework selection decision will need to be revisited as the local agent architecture matures. Track alternatives, but do not switch frameworks without a documented evaluation and migration plan.

---

## Implementation checklist

- [ ] Set a structured research schedule — 30 minutes, three sessions per week, calendar-protected
- [ ] Conduct a monthly local model benchmark evaluation — consistent test suite, documented results
- [ ] Track MCP compatibility for all current and prospective tools
- [ ] Monitor EU AI Act guidance updates — reassess AI Register within 30 days of any new guidance
- [ ] Evaluate one new AI-assisted security tool per month against the vendor scorecard
- [ ] Maintain a research log in OpenBrain — tag findings with `research` and relevant topic tags
